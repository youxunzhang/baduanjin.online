八段锦八个动作详解

学习八段锦口诀和标准动作要领，掌握传统健身气功功法

Complete Practice Sequence

Each form in Ba Duan Jin targets specific organs and energy meridians. Practice them in sequence for maximum benefit, focusing on smooth transitions and natural breathing.

🧘

Start with 3-5 repetitions of each form

⏱️

Hold each position for 5-10 seconds

🌬️

Coordinate movement with natural breathing

01

Two Hands Hold Up the Heavens

Shuang Shou Tuo Tian Li San Jiao

This opening form regulates the triple heater (san jiao) and promotes overall qi circulation. The upward stretching motion helps align the spine and opens the chest.

Benefits:

  • Improves posture and spinal alignment
  • Enhances respiratory function
  • Regulates internal organ function
  • Reduces shoulder and neck tension

Instructions:

  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart
  2. Interlace fingers and raise hands above head
  3. Turn palms upward as if holding up the sky
  4. Gently stretch upward, then lower arms to sides
02

Drawing the Bow to Shoot an Eagle

Zuo You Kai Gong Si She Diao

This form strengthens the arms and shoulders while improving focus and concentration. The bow-drawing motion enhances lung capacity and chest expansion.

Benefits:

  • Strengthens arms, shoulders, and chest
  • Improves lung capacity and breathing
  • Enhances focus and mental clarity
  • Regulates kidney and lung meridians

Instructions:

  1. Stand in horse stance with arms crossed
  2. Draw left arm back as if pulling a bowstring
  3. Extend right arm forward, pointing index finger
  4. Hold briefly, then switch sides
03

Separate Heaven and Earth

Tiao Li Pi Wei Xu Dan Ju

This form regulates the spleen and stomach, improving digestion and metabolism. The opposing movements help balance the body's energy systems.

Benefits:

  • Improves digestive function
  • Regulates spleen and stomach qi
  • Enhances metabolic processes
  • Balances upper and lower body energy

Instructions:

  1. Raise one hand overhead, palm facing up
  2. Press other hand downward, palm facing down
  3. Feel the stretch through your torso
  4. Switch hand positions and repeat
04

Wise Owl Gazes Backwards

Wu Lao Qi Shang Wang Hou Qiao

This form addresses the five weaknesses and seven injuries by promoting flexibility in the neck and spine while calming the nervous system.

Benefits:

  • Improves neck and spine flexibility
  • Reduces stress and anxiety
  • Enhances nervous system function
  • Promotes mental clarity and focus

Instructions:

  1. Stand with hands on hips
  2. Slowly turn head to look over left shoulder
  3. Keep shoulders relaxed and level
  4. Return to center, then turn to right
05

Sway the Head and Shake the Tail

Yao Tou Bai Wei Qu Xin Huo

This form helps clear heart fire and reduce emotional stress. The swaying motion promotes flexibility throughout the spine and releases tension.

Benefits:

  • Reduces emotional stress and anxiety
  • Improves spinal flexibility
  • Regulates heart and kidney energy
  • Promotes emotional balance

Instructions:

  1. Stand in wide horse stance
  2. Place hands on knees
  3. Sway upper body in circular motions
  4. Keep movements smooth and controlled
06

Two Hands Hold the Feet

Liang Shou Pan Zu Gu Shen Yao

This form strengthens the kidneys and lower back while improving flexibility in the legs and spine. It's particularly beneficial for kidney and bladder health.

Benefits:

  • Strengthens kidneys and lower back
  • Improves leg and spine flexibility
  • Enhances circulation in lower body
  • Supports kidney and bladder function

Instructions:

  1. Stand with feet together
  2. Bend forward and reach toward feet
  3. Keep knees slightly bent
  4. Slowly return to standing position
07

Clench Fists and Glare Fiercely

Zan Quan Nu Mu Zeng Qi Li

This form builds internal strength and qi power. The fierce expression and clenched fists help cultivate determination and mental fortitude.

Benefits:

  • Builds internal strength and power
  • Enhances mental determination
  • Improves circulation and qi flow
  • Strengthens liver and gallbladder energy

Instructions:

  1. Stand in horse stance
  2. Clench fists at waist level
  3. Punch forward with fierce expression
  4. Alternate left and right punches
08

Bouncing on the Toes

Bei Hou Qi Dian Bai Bing Xiao

This closing form helps eliminate diseases and restore balance. The bouncing motion stimulates all meridians and promotes overall health.

Benefits:

  • Stimulates all meridian points
  • Promotes overall health and vitality
  • Improves balance and coordination
  • Integrates the benefits of all previous forms

Instructions:

  1. Stand with feet parallel
  2. Rise up on toes, then drop heels
  3. Let the bounce travel through your body
  4. Repeat 7-10 times to conclude practice
